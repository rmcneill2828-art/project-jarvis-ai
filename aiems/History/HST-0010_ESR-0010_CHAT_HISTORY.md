7. Guardian UXP Design Outcome

One of the most important design outcomes of ESR-0010 was the approval of the Guardian UXP Orb direction. The design emerged from the observation that the Obsidian knowledge graph already resembled a living representation of Project JARVIS AI knowledge and architecture.

Approved UXP Principle

The interface is a visualisation of the platform's real operational state, not a decorative dashboard.

The Guardian Orb is the long-term visual centre of the Guardian User Experience Platform.

The Orb is inspired by Obsidian knowledge graph representation.

Nodes represent real artefacts, capabilities, systems or agents.

Connections represent real relationships.

Architectural clusters illuminate as systems are accessed.

New agents appear as white nodes and connect into the graph when they come online.

Status rings around the Orb represent Guardian or platform operational state.

Side panes provide contextual engineering and operational information.

A persistent chat bar beneath the Orb remains the primary human interaction surface.

The UXP grows with platform capability, not ahead of it.

The Guardian UXP design direction is approved as a vision and architectural direction. It is not yet an implementation package. Future implementation should proceed only when supporting platform capabilities exist.
