# Purpose

This Strategic Alignment Review (SAR) confirms the successful completion of **Phase 0 – Foundation**, evaluates the current strategic position of the AI Engineering Platform (AEP), and determines whether the programme is ready to proceed to **Phase 1 – Engineering Readiness**.

The review ensures that the programme remains aligned with the Platform Charter, Engineering Constitution and Platform Architecture Model before authorising the next phase.

---

# Review Scope

This review considers:

- Achievement of Phase 0 objectives
- Engineering governance maturity
- Repository integrity
- Architectural readiness
- Strategic alignment
- Programme priorities
- Risks and opportunities
- Readiness to commence Phase 1

---

# Key Achievements

Significant achievements during Phase 0 include:

- Establishment of the AI Engineering Platform (AEP)
- Creation of the AI Engineering Management System (AIEMS)
- Approval of the Platform Charter and Engineering Constitution
- Creation of the initial Architecture Decision Records
- Establishment of the Controlled Artefact Register
- Reconstruction of the Platform Architecture Model
- Adoption of a disciplined Engineering Assurance Review process

These achievements provide a stable and governed foundation for future engineering activities.

The successful completion of Phase 0 demonstrates that the AI Engineering Platform has established an effective governance, architectural and engineering foundation.

The purpose of this Strategic Alignment Review is therefore not to determine whether Phase 0 was successful, but to determine whether the programme is sufficiently prepared to authorise the commencement of Phase 1 – Engineering Readiness.
---

# Strategic Alignment Assessment

The Programme Sponsor and Chief Engineering Advisor jointly assessed the current strategic position of the AI Engineering Platform against the objectives defined within the Platform Charter.

## Vision Alignment

The original programme vision remains valid.

During Phase 0, the programme matured from a single AI project into the AI Engineering Platform comprising:

- AI Engineering Management System (AIEMS)
- JARVIS

This evolution strengthens rather than changes the original vision.

**Assessment:** ✅ Aligned

---

## Governance Assessment

The governance framework has matured significantly.

Phase 0 established:

- Constitutional governance
- Operational governance
- Engineering Assurance Reviews
- Repository Baselines
- Controlled Artefact management

The Platform is now governed through AIEMS.

**Assessment:** ✅ Satisfactory

---

## Architecture Assessment

The Platform Architecture Model provides a stable architectural baseline.

The relationship between AIEMS and JARVIS has been clearly defined.

Repository structure now reflects Platform architecture.

**Assessment:** ✅ Ready

---

## Technology Assessment

No significant technology changes have been identified that require alteration of the Platform vision or architecture.

The decision to defer AI engineering toolchain integration until a future Architecture Decision Record is considered appropriate.

**Assessment:** ✅ No change required

---

# Lessons Learned

Phase 0 produced several important engineering lessons.

## Governance Before Implementation

Establishing governance before implementation significantly reduced ambiguity and improved engineering consistency.

---

## Repository Structure Matters

Aligning the repository with the Platform architecture simplified documentation, traceability and long-term maintainability.

---

## Engineering Assurance Adds Value

Engineering Assurance Reviews improved quality by identifying inconsistencies before they became part of the approved baseline.

---

## Evidence Improves Decisions

The principle:

> **Assume Nothing. Verify Everything.**

proved valuable throughout Phase 0 and will remain a guiding principle for future phases.

# Phase 1 Success Criteria

Phase 1 – Engineering Readiness shall be considered successfully completed when the following outcomes have been achieved.

| Objective | Success Measure |
|-----------|-----------------|
| AIEMS Standards | Core standards approved and baselined |
| Engineering Toolchain | Reproducible development environment established |
| Engineering Automation | Automated quality assurance integrated into the engineering workflow |
| JARVIS Core Skeleton | Platform architecture represented as executable project structure |
| Engineering Readiness | Repository can be cloned, built, validated and tested consistently |
| Engineering Knowledge | Engineering guidance and reusable development practices established |

Achievement of these outcomes will demonstrate that the AI Engineering Platform possesses the engineering capability required to commence product-focused development.

---

# Strategic Risks

The following strategic risks were considered during this review.

| Risk | Assessment | Mitigation |
|------|------------|------------|
| Loss of engineering discipline | Low | Continue operating under AIEMS governance |
| Premature feature development | Medium | Prioritise engineering readiness before functionality |
| Technology change | Low | Continue Strategic Alignment Reviews before each programme phase |
| Repository inconsistency | Low | Maintain Engineering Assurance Reviews and controlled artefact management |

No strategic risks were identified that would prevent progression to Phase 1.

# Strategic Decision

Following completion of this Strategic Alignment Review, the Programme Sponsor concludes that:

- Phase 0 has successfully achieved its stated objectives.
- The AI Engineering Platform has established a governed engineering foundation.
- The approved Phase 0 engineering baseline represents a stable and controlled engineering baseline.
- The Platform remains aligned with its strategic vision.
- The proposed objectives for Phase 1 directly support the long-term success of the programme.

The Programme Sponsor therefore authorises the commencement of:
