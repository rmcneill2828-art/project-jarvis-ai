# 1. Purpose

HABEI-0001 preserves initial research evidence about Human-AI engineering behaviour observed across Project JARVIS AI and AIEMS engineering sessions.

The investigation supports future decisions about behavioural continuity, WP0 improvement, AI-assisted engineering reliability and long-term JARVIS behavioural capability.

This artefact is a research artefact. It is not a controlled AIEMS governance artefact unless later promoted through separate approval.

---

# 2. Background

Project JARVIS AI has developed through repeated Human-AI engineering sessions in which governance, implementation discipline and repository-first behaviour matured through observed practice.

Across the initial project session and Engineering Sessions ESR-0001 through ESR-0003, the collaboration showed evidence of emerging engineering restraint, role separation, recovery behaviour and process learning.

HABEI-0001 records that evidence so it can inform future review without changing AIEMS governance during this implementation.

# 5. Methodology

The investigation uses session-based qualitative review.

Each Behavioural Evidence Record records:

- Session reviewed.
- Key behavioural findings.
- Outcome.
- Research status.
- Boundary between evidence and recommendation.

Findings are descriptive and evidential. They are not implementation instructions.

---

# 6. Key Investigation Question

Can observed Human-AI engineering behaviour across Project JARVIS AI sessions provide useful evidence for future AIEMS behavioural continuity, WP0 improvement and JARVIS behavioural capability decisions?

---

# 7. Initial Conclusions

Initial evidence suggests that AIEMS principles emerged from observed engineering practice rather than from abstract process design alone.

Repository-first thinking, engineering restraint, role separation and recovery behaviour appear to strengthen over repeated sessions.

ESR-0003 also shows that behavioural drift can occur early in a session, and that recovery behaviour can improve significantly when the AI collaborator is corrected and re-anchored to AIEMS expectations.

These conclusions are research findings only. They do not modify AIEMS.
