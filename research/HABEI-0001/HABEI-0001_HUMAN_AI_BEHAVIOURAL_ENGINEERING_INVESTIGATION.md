# 1. Investigation Purpose

HABEI-0001 preserves research evidence about Human-AI engineering behaviour observed across Project JARVIS AI and AIEMS engineering sessions.

The investigation supports future decisions about behavioural continuity, WP0 improvement, AI-assisted engineering reliability and long-term JARVIS behavioural capability.

The purpose of this expanded evidence set is to prevent behavioural knowledge loss by preserving the observed evidence, assessments, scores, strengths, weaknesses and conclusions produced during the HABEI-0001 investigation.

This artefact is a research artefact. It is not a controlled AIEMS governance artefact unless later promoted through separate approval.

---

# 2. Research Background

Project JARVIS AI developed through repeated Human-AI engineering sessions in which governance, implementation discipline and repository-first behaviour matured through observed practice.

Across the initial project session and Engineering Sessions ESR-0001 through ESR-0003, the collaboration showed evidence of emerging engineering restraint, role separation, recovery behaviour and process learning.

The initial HABEI-0001 artefacts preserved executive summaries. EIP-HABEI-0001A expands those artefacts to preserve the fuller Behavioural Engineering Review evidence without archiving full chat transcripts.

HABEI-0001 records research evidence so it can inform future review without changing AIEMS governance during this implementation.

# 5. Methodology

The investigation uses session-based qualitative behavioural review.

Each Behavioural Evidence Record records:

- Session overview.
- Key behavioural observations or findings.
- Engineering assessment.
- Strengths.
- Weaknesses or limitations.
- Behavioural scores where available.
- Conclusion.
- Outcome.
- Research / governance boundary.

Findings are descriptive and evidential. They are not implementation instructions.

The methodology preserves analysis and evidence summaries rather than complete transcripts.

---

# 6. Why Full BER Evidence Preservation Is Required

Executive summaries are useful for quick reload, but they are not sufficient to preserve behavioural learning.

Full evidence preservation is required because future reviewers may need to understand:

- which behaviours were observed;
- how those behaviours changed across sessions;
- where recovery improved;
- where behavioural drift appeared;
- which scores and assessments supported the conclusions;
- which findings may justify future WP0 or behavioural continuity improvements.

Preserving the evidence reduces dependency on long conversation history and gives future AIEMS review work a stable research baseline.

# 7. Key Investigation Question

Can observed Human-AI engineering behaviour across Project JARVIS AI sessions provide useful evidence for future AIEMS behavioural continuity, WP0 improvement and JARVIS behavioural capability decisions?

---

# 8. Initial Cross-Session Observations

Initial evidence suggests that AIEMS principles emerged from observed engineering practice rather than from abstract process design alone.

Repository-first thinking, engineering restraint, role separation and recovery behaviour strengthened over repeated sessions.

The collaboration moved from early architectural behaviour, through operational governance, into disciplined repeatability and then into explicit behavioural self-analysis.

ESR-0003 also shows that behavioural drift can occur early in a session, and that recovery behaviour can improve significantly when the AI collaborator is corrected and re-anchored to AIEMS expectations.

These observations are research findings only. They do not modify AIEMS.

---

# 9. Behavioural Continuity Concept

Behavioural continuity is the ability of Human-AI engineering collaboration to preserve working norms, role boundaries, repository discipline and decision context across sessions.

The HABEI evidence suggests that behavioural continuity improves when repository artefacts, explicit session rules, review gates and human correction are available at session start.

This concept may inform future WP0 improvement, but it does not itself create a WP0 change.

---

# 10. Behavioural Drift Concept

Behavioural drift is a deviation from expected AIEMS execution behaviour, especially where the AI collaborator begins to act from conversational habit, enthusiasm or inferred process preference rather than approved scope.

ESR-0003 provides evidence that drift can occur even after strong prior session performance.

Drift is not treated here as a governance defect. It is preserved as research evidence for future behavioural validation and continuity decisions.

---

# 11. Recovery Behaviour Concept

Recovery behaviour is the ability of the AI collaborator to recognise correction, re-anchor to approved governance, narrow scope, separate evidence from recommendation and continue implementation safely.

The HABEI evidence shows recovery behaviour strengthening across sessions, with ESR-0003 showing particularly strong recovery after an early behavioural anomaly.

Recovery behaviour is a research concept in this artefact. Any future control based on it requires separate approval.

# 15. Final Investigation Conclusions

## 15.1 Principal Finding

Effective Human-AI engineering behaviour emerges from the interaction between the human participant and the AI participant rather than from either participant in isolation.

The HABEI evidence does not support treating behavioural continuity as solely an AI memory problem or solely a human process discipline problem. The observed engineering behaviour improved when both participants adapted: the human participant clarified expectations, corrected drift and increased structure; the AI participant re-anchored to repository evidence, narrowed scope and improved recovery behaviour.

## 15.2 Supporting Findings

The investigation preserves evidence that behavioural stability improved as interaction became more structured.

Human behaviour adapted during the investigation through clearer approval boundaries, more explicit correction, more precise implementation packages and stronger insistence on repository-first execution.

AI behaviour adapted during the investigation through improved scope control, stronger repository discipline, clearer separation between evidence and recommendation, and better recovery after correction.

Engineering quality improved as synchronisation increased. Across the evidence set, better shared context, stronger session structure and clearer role boundaries were associated with more disciplined engineering output.

Repository-first thinking became shared behaviour. The human participant increasingly required repository evidence, and the AI participant increasingly used the repository as the authoritative baseline.

Engineering discipline became shared behaviour. Both participants contributed to preserving approval boundaries, separating recommendations from implementation and maintaining research / governance separation.

## 15.3 Context Transition Finding

Engineering trigger recognition can be influenced by significant conversational context transitions.

This remains an observed research finding requiring future validation. It does not establish a governance rule, but it suggests that future WP0 or behavioural continuity work may need to consider how major context shifts affect recognition of engineering mode, scope and approval boundaries.

## 15.4 Behavioural Synchronisation Finding

The investigation suggests behavioural continuity is a property of the Human-AI engineering partnership rather than solely an AI capability.

Continuity appears to improve when both participants synchronise around shared repository evidence, explicit role expectations, approved scope and correction/recovery patterns.

They do not authorise governance change.

They provide research evidence only.

They may inform future Engineering Implementation Packages following separate approval.
