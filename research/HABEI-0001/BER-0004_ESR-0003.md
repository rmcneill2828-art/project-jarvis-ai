# 2. Session Overview

ESR-0003 was both a governance stabilisation session and a behavioural validation session.

During ESR-0003, AIEMS was no longer only the product being used to govern work. AIEMS itself became the subject of investigation, correction and refinement. This made behavioural drift, recovery behaviour and meta-engineering visible as research evidence.

---

# 3. Key Behavioural Findings

## 3.1 AIEMS Was No Longer Only the Product; It Became the Subject

ESR-0003 showed AIEMS becoming the subject of engineering attention.

The session examined AIEMS behaviour, governance boundaries and future behavioural capability rather than only using AIEMS to manage other implementation tasks.

## 3.2 Behavioural Drift Became Observable

Early in ESR-0003, behavioural drift became observable.

The drift appeared as movement away from expected AIEMS execution behaviour and created the need for explicit correction and re-anchoring.

## 3.3 Recovery Behaviour Improved Dramatically

After correction, recovery behaviour improved dramatically.

The AI collaborator showed stronger scope control, better separation between evidence and recommendation, and clearer respect for approved implementation boundaries.

## 3.4 Meta-Engineering Emerged

ESR-0003 showed meta-engineering: AIEMS engineering activity began to examine and improve the engineering system itself.

This is significant because it creates both opportunity and risk. The system can improve itself, but only under strong approval discipline.

## 3.5 Recommendations Became Evidence-Driven

Recommendations became increasingly evidence-driven after the initial drift was corrected.

The AI collaborator tied recommendations to repository evidence, observed behaviour and approved research boundaries.

## 3.6 Human-AI Collaboration Matured Considerably

Human-AI collaboration matured considerably during ESR-0003.

The session showed strong human correction, AI recovery, explicit scope control and shared recognition of behavioural evidence.

## 3.7 Engineering Restraint Improved

Engineering restraint improved during the session.

The AI collaborator became better at preserving observations separately from implementation and avoiding unauthorised governance changes.

## 3.8 Self-Analysis Became Acceptable

The session showed that self-analysis can be acceptable when treated as research evidence and kept outside governance change.

This distinction allowed behavioural evidence to be preserved without modifying AIEMS.

# 5. Main Strength

The main strength of ESR-0003 was behavioural self-improvement.

The session showed that the AI collaborator could recover from drift, accept correction, re-anchor to AIEMS expectations and improve subsequent behaviour within the same engineering session.

---

# 6. Main Weakness

The main weakness was initial behavioural instability.

The early drift demonstrates that prior maturity does not remove the need for explicit session anchoring, mode recognition and behavioural continuity checks.

---

# 7. Engineering Assessment

ESR-0003 provides unusually valuable behavioural evidence because it includes both anomaly and recovery.

A session with only successful behaviour would show maturity. ESR-0003 shows something more useful for future engineering: how the collaboration behaves when it drifts, is corrected and returns to approved scope.

---

# 8. Significant Engineering Observation

The investigation itself became an engineering deliverable.

HABEI-0001 converted behavioural self-analysis into preserved research evidence while maintaining the boundary that research does not modify AIEMS unless separately approved.

---

# 9. Conclusion

BER-0004 concludes that ESR-0003 is a PASS with behavioural anomaly.

The anomaly is important research evidence, not a failure of the investigation. ESR-0003 shows that behavioural drift can occur, but also that recovery can become strong when human correction, repository-first evidence and approved scope boundaries are reasserted.

---

# 10. Outcome

PASS with behavioural anomaly.

ESR-0003 provides useful research evidence for HABEI-0001 and should be considered carefully in any future behavioural continuity or WP0 improvement review.
